For Publishers

Benefits

  • Confidence that their documents are being used according to their license agreements
  • Supporting the Green Way of Open Access
  • Help institutions accurately capture and respect your licensing terms, including any embargo periods.

How to join

Please contact us at info@oa-deepgreen.org if you would like to find out more.

Please note that DeepGreen will not create one account for each publisher but one account for each alliance license contract. If a publisher is part of more than one contract, they will receive more than one account.

How it works

The Publications Router enables publishers to automatically deposit publications in corresponding Open Access repositories. Publishers send publications and their metadata to the Publications Router which then finds corresponding repositories and notifies them about the publication.

The Publications Router provides the following interfaces for delivering metadata and the full text:

  • SWORD2: according to the SWORD V2 service document (here: NISO JATS and PDF/WORD),
  • FTP: JATS-XML for metadata, plus zip-files for full text and other files
  • Publicatons Router API: specific JSON structure.

Next to the basic transfer functions of JISC Publications Router, DeepGreen will adjust these to fit German conditions.

The publications are assigned to institutions with affiliation indicators that should be included in the metadata: name of the institution, author name(s) with ORCID if available, email adress of the author(s), etc.

The institutions are obligated to respect any embargo periods and other license regulations.

FAQ

+ -
What is the DeepGreen Publications Router?

The DeepGreen Publications Router is a platform which enables publishers to comfortably deliver publications with alliance licenses to Open Access repositories and support the Green Way of Open Access. Publishers send so called notifications to DeepGreen which matches them to corresponding institutions and provides them for publication.

+ -
What are notifications?

Notifications are publications (.pdf) with their metadata and, if necessary, secondary items such as image files. Each notification is a .zip file containing one publication.

+ -
What are notifications?

Notifications are publications (.pdf) with their metadata and, if necessary, secondary items such as image files. Each notification is a .zip file containing one publication.

+ -
How are notifications sent?

Notifications are sent either using a SWORD or SFTP interface. To send notifications please refer to your account details where you will find all the necessary details to access either SWORD or SFTP.

+ -
Will the DeepGreen Publications Router store publications?

The router will not store or save any publications. Publications that can be matched to an institution will be delivered and unmatched publications will be deleted from the system.

+ -
Why are the accounts created for contracts and not for individual publishers?

Each publisher can have several alliance licenses with different licensing and embargo terms. Therefore accounts that represent the agreed upon terms are the better solution and ensure compliance with the licensing terms

+ -
How can I create an account?

Please contact the DeepGreen team (info@oa-deepgreen.de) and an account will be created for you (or more than one, depending on the contracts). The login information will be sent to you. After your first login, we advise you to change your password in your account details. There you can also provide additional information under the “Configure Settings” headlines. Please note that this is optional.

+ -
How does DeepGreen match notifications to institutions?

Institutions provide a comprehensive list with possible names for the institutions, names of authors with (if available) ORCIDs and their domain names. This is the basis for a successful matching process. DeepGreen compares this information to affiliation records found in the metadata of notifications.

+ -
Where can I review the publications that have been sent?

In your account details you will see a link titled "View routed notifications for this accoutn (API)" which lets you review your sent notifications.

+ -
Where does the information for the alliance licenses come from?

DeepGreen collaborates with the Elektronische Zeitschriftenbibliothek (EZB) and receives the license information through them. An alliance licenses contract includes certain journals which can be identified using an EZB ID or a ZDB ID if it is a journal collection. DeepGreen uses these IDs to include licensing terms in the metadata of notifications sent to the institutions.
